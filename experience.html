Experience

Taking every opportunity to build my career.

My professional journey has been shaped by diverse experiences that have developed my leadership, analytical, and interpersonal skills. Each role has contributed uniquely to my growth and understanding of different aspects of business and human interaction.

From 2021 to 2023, I served as a full-time missionary for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in Villahermosa, Mexico. This immersive experience not only helped me become fluent in Spanish but also developed my abilities in project management, leadership, and cross-cultural communication. Working with local communities and coordinating with fellow missionaries, I learned valuable lessons about adapting to new environments, setting and achieving goals, and building meaningful relationships across cultural boundaries.

Prior to my university studies, I had the opportunity to work as a research assistant at the University of Pennsylvania, where I supported faculty research in market analysis and economic forecasting. This experience introduced me to the rigors of academic research and data analysis, skills that continue to serve me well in my current studies. Working directly with professors and graduate students, I gained hands-on experience with data collection, statistical analysis, and research methodology, while contributing to meaningful academic work in the field of economics.
